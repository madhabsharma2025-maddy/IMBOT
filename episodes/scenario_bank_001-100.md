# Family Sitcom — Episode Scenario Bank (Batch 1: 100 episodes)

## Character Bible (quick reference)

- **Mom** — fast, sharp, intelligent; solves problems quickly, sometimes too quickly, which is its own running joke.
- **Dad** — slow, honest to a fault; never lies even when it'd be easier, gets things done the long but right way.
- **Son** — agreeable, listens to everyone; growth arc = learning when to have his own opinion.
- **Daughter** — clever, resourceful; often the one who spots the real problem first.
- **Fox** — the moral center; every episode ends with Fox delivering the lesson/motto in a warm, simple line.
- **Rabbit** — proud, sarcastic, "doesn't care"; learns the lesson every episode, resets to attitude by next episode (the comedy is in the relapse).
- **Panda** — easygoing goofball; physical comedy, fart jokes, chaos-maker who lightens tense moments.
- **Snake** — elegant, graceful, unfailingly positive; the helper/peacemaker, often the one who reframes a problem kindly.
- **Monkey** — small, messy, mischievous; makes a mess wherever he goes but is instantly forgiven because he's tiny and lovable.

## Format note
Each entry = **Title | Theme | One-line hook | How it lands**. "Lands" = the beat where Fox states the lesson and Rabbit rolls his eyes back into his ego by the tag scene.

### Honesty & Integrity
1. **The Broken Vase** | Owning mistakes | Monkey breaks Mom's favorite vase; everyone suspects each other until Dad's honesty forces a family confession circle. | Fox: "The truth is heavier to hold than to say."
2. **Panda's Big Fib** | White lies | Panda claims he did a chore he didn't, to look good in front of Snake. | Fox: "Being liked for a lie feels good for a minute, and bad for a week."
3. **The Borrowed Bike** | Returning what isn't yours | Son borrows Rabbit's bike without asking and dents it. | Fox: "Asking first is respect. Confessing after is just cleanup."
4. **Test Day Trouble** | Cheating temptation | Daughter is offered answers before an exam. | Fox: "A copied answer teaches you nothing except how to feel guilty."
5. **The Mystery Cookie Thief** | Admitting fault | Someone ate the cookies meant for a guest — turns out it was Dad. | Fox: "Grown-ups mess up too. Saying sorry doesn't shrink with age."

### Kindness & Empathy
6. **New Kid in the Burrow** | Welcoming outsiders | A new animal moves in nearby and Rabbit is cold to him until he sees himself in the kid's loneliness. | Fox: "Everyone was new somewhere once."
7. **Snake's Quiet Friend** | Understanding shyness | A shy animal won't speak at the picnic; Snake patiently draws him out instead of forcing him. | Fox: "Kindness doesn't need to be loud to be heard."
8. **The Empty Lunchbox** | Sharing | Daughter notices a classmate has no lunch and quietly starts sharing hers. | Fox: "You don't need permission to be generous."
9. **Panda's Practical Joke** | Consequences of teasing | A prank on Monkey goes too far and hurts his feelings. | Fox: "Funny for one person isn't always funny for both."
10. **The Grumpy Neighbor** | Patience with difficult people | An easily annoyed neighbor turns out to be lonely, not mean. | Fox: "Some prickly people just need someone to stay a little longer."

### Family & Cooperation
11. **The Leaky Roof** | Working together | A storm floods the house and everyone must pitch in with a different skill. | Fox: "A team fixes faster than a hero."
12. **Mom's Day Off** | Appreciating unseen work | Mom stops doing chores for a day so the family can see how much she does. | Fox: "Notice the work before it's missing."
13. **Dad's Slow Fix** | Doing it right vs. doing it fast | Dad insists on repairing the fence properly while everyone wants a quick patch; the quick patch fails first. | Fox: "Slow and solid outlasts fast and flimsy."
14. **Divide the Chores** | Fairness | The kids argue over who does more work until they trade jobs for a day. | Fox: "Walk in someone's chores before you judge their effort."
15. **The Family Vote** | Compromise | The family can't agree on a weekend activity; they learn to blend ideas instead of picking a winner. | Fox: "A good compromise makes everyone a little happy, not one person fully happy."

### Ego, Pride & Rabbit's Arc
16. **Rabbit's Race** | Humility | Rabbit brags he'll win the hill race easily and stumbles from overconfidence. | Fox: "Confidence without care trips over itself." (Rabbit, muttering: "I still would've won.")
17. **The Trophy That Wasn't His** | Sharing credit | Rabbit takes full credit for a group project win. | Fox: "Credit shared is a gift; credit stolen is a debt."
18. **Rabbit vs. the Mirror** | Self-image | Rabbit avoids Snake's advice out of pride and makes the problem worse alone. | Fox: "Pride can talk you out of the exact help you need."
19. **The Apology Rabbit Wouldn't Give** | Saying sorry | Rabbit is clearly wrong but won't admit it until it costs him a friendship for a day. | Fox: "Sorry is a small word that buys back a lot of trust."
20. **Rabbit Babysits Monkey** | Patience | Rabbit is forced to watch chaotic Monkey for the day and discovers a soft side he denies having by dinner. | Fox: "Even the toughest heart has a soft corner — Rabbit just guards his well."

### Panda's Comedy Beats (with a lesson underneath)
21. **The Great Fart Contest** | Knowing your audience | Panda's silly contest with Monkey ruins a fancy dinner party. | Fox: "Fun has a time and a place — the trick is spotting which."
22. **Panda's Nap Emergency** | Responsibility vs. rest | Panda oversleeps through his one job (watching the gate) and something goes missing. | Fox: "Rest is important — but so is remembering what you promised."
23. **Panda Tries to Be Serious** | Being yourself | Panda tries to act "proper" to impress someone and it backfires; his real goofy self was what everyone liked. | Fox: "The version of you people love is usually the real one."
24. **The Tickle Trap** | Boundaries in play | A game of tickling goes on after Daughter says stop. | Fox: "'Stop' ends the game, even a fun one."

### Snake's Grace & Positivity
25. **Snake's Bad Day** | Positivity isn't pretending | Snake has a genuinely rough day and the family learns even the "always positive" one needs comfort sometimes. | Fox: "Being positive doesn't mean never being sad — it means not staying there alone."
26. **The Beauty Contest** | Inner vs. outer beauty | Snake is praised only for her looks and quietly corrects the compliment. | Fox: "What you did matters more than how you looked doing it."
27. **Snake Makes Peace** | Conflict resolution | Snake mediates a fight between Rabbit and Panda without taking sides. | Fox: "You don't have to agree with both sides to help both sides."

### Monkey's Messes (with heart)
28. **Monkey Redecorates** | Cleaning up after yourself | Monkey "helps" clean the house and makes it worse; learns to ask before "helping." | Fox: "Good intentions still need good instructions."
29. **The Great Banana Heist** | Consequences of greed | Monkey hoards all the bananas and gets sick, then shares what's left. | Fox: "Taking more than you need leaves less than everyone needs."
30. **Monkey's Time-Out** | Understanding consequences, not punishment as cruelty | Monkey is upset about a fair time-out until Fox helps him see why rules exist. | Fox: "A rule isn't a cage — it's a fence that keeps you safe."

### School, Learning & Growing Up
31. **Son's Group Project** | Speaking up | Son goes along with a bad group idea because he "always listens to everyone." | Fox: "Listening to others shouldn't mean silencing yourself."
32. **The Spelling Bee Loss** | Losing gracefully | Daughter loses a competition to a friend and struggles with jealousy before congratulating her. | Fox: "Someone else's win doesn't erase your effort."
33. **Homework Excuses** | Time management | Son procrastinates and learns to plan instead of panic. | Fox: "Tomorrow's homework doesn't do itself while you wait."
34. **The Bully at the Pond** | Standing up for others | Son watches a smaller animal get teased and must decide whether to speak up. | Fox: "Silence next to unkindness looks a lot like agreement."
35. **Daughter's Invention Fails** | Failure as part of learning | Daughter's science project flops publicly; she learns failure isn't the opposite of success, it's part of it. | Fox: "Every invention that works stood on a pile of ones that didn't."

### Health, Habits & Everyday Life
36. **The Vegetable Standoff** | Healthy eating without lectures | Kids refuse vegetables until Panda turns cooking into a game. | Fox: "You can't force a taste, but you can make it fun to try."
37. **Screen Time Standoff** | Balance | The kids ignore playtime for screens until a power cut forces old-fashioned fun — which they end up loving. | Fox: "Balance isn't giving something up, it's making room for more."
38. **The Late-Night Fear** | Facing fears | Son is scared of the dark; Dad doesn't mock it, just sits with him until it shrinks. | Fox: "Fear gets smaller when it's not faced alone."
39. **Wash Your Hands, Wash Your Excuses** | Hygiene | A mini "germs are invisible guests" episode after everyone in the house catches a cold from skipped handwashing. | Fox: "Small habits are invisible shields."
40. **The Grumpy Morning** | Managing emotions | Everyone wakes up cranky and takes it out on each other until Snake breaks the cycle with one kind act. | Fox: "A bad mood is contagious — so is a good one."

### Money, Sharing & Values
41. **The Piggy Bank Problem** | Saving vs. spending | Daughter wants to spend all her saved coins on candy; learns to save for something bigger. | Fox: "A coin saved today is a wish kept for tomorrow."
42. **Monkey's Trade** | Value of things | Monkey trades something valuable for something shiny and worthless, then learns to look past shine. | Fox: "Shiny isn't the same as valuable."
43. **The Charity Drive** | Giving without expecting | The family collects old toys to give away; Son wants credit/recognition for it. | Fox: "Giving quietly is still giving fully."
44. **Rabbit's Reluctant Donation** | Letting go of things | Rabbit refuses to give up his old, outgrown toys until he sees another kid's joy. | Fox: "What you've outgrown might be someone else's treasure."

### Environment & Nature
45. **The Littered Forest** | Environmental responsibility | The animals' picnic spot is trashed by visitors; the family organizes a clean-up. | Fox: "We all live downstream from someone's mess — including our own."
46. **Save the Bees** | Ecosystem awareness | Panda wants to swat every bee until Fox explains their role. | Fox: "Even the things that scare us can be keeping us fed."
47. **The Water Wasters** | Conservation | A leaking tap goes unfixed for days; Dad finally repairs it and shows the wasted-water math. | Fox: "A small drip, over time, is still a flood."
48. **Planting Season** | Patience and long-term effort | Daughter plants a seed and wants it to grow overnight. | Fox: "Good things grow at their own pace, not ours."

### Friendship & Social Skills
49. **The Sleepover Fight** | Conflict resolution among friends | Rabbit and Panda fight at a sleepover over whose game to play. | Fox: "Taking turns isn't losing — it's sharing the fun."
50. **The Secret That Wasn't Kept** | Trust | Someone shares a friend's secret and has to earn trust back. | Fox: "Trust is a glass — cracked once, it's never quite the same shape again, but it can still hold water."
51. **New Best Friend Jealousy** | Jealousy in friendships | Daughter feels replaced when her friend makes another friend. | Fox: "Friendship isn't a cup that only holds one."
52. **The Group Chat Drama** | Miscommunication | A misunderstood message causes a rift between the kids and their friends. | Fox: "Words without tone can be misread — check before you're hurt."
53. **Snake Includes Everyone** | Inclusion | A game is about to leave Monkey out for being "too small"; Snake redesigns the rules so he can play. | Fox: "Change the game before you change who gets to play."

### Big Feelings
54. **Son's Silent Sadness** | Recognizing others' feelings | Son is quietly upset and no one notices until Fox teaches everyone to "check in," not just "check on." | Fox: "Being present is noticing before someone has to ask for help."
55. **Rabbit's Bad Day, Big Reaction** | Managing anger | Rabbit lashes out at everyone over a small mistake and has to make amends. | Fox: "Anger borrows trouble from tomorrow to spend today."
56. **The Comparison Trap** | Self-worth | Daughter compares herself to a "perfect" classmate constantly. | Fox: "You're not behind in someone else's race — you're running your own."
57. **Panda's Fear of Failing** | Trying despite fear | Panda avoids trying a new skill in front of others out of fear of looking silly. | Fox: "Looking silly while trying beats never finding out."

### Community & Respect
58. **Respecting Elders** | Elder respect | The family visits an elderly neighbor and the kids learn patience and the value of listening to stories. | Fox: "Old stories are maps drawn by people who've already walked the road."
59. **The New Rules at School** | Respecting authority fairly | Kids resist a new school rule until they understand its purpose. | Fox: "A rule makes more sense once you know why it's there."
60. **Helping the Repairman** | Respect for all work | The family thanks and assists a repair worker instead of just watching him work. | Fox: "No job is small to the person depending on it being done well."
